Influence of Different Factors on Flight Delays in the Period of 1992-2004¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation we're looking at how the flight delays changed within 12 years (picking up 3 years) and what influences on them. We also want to verify the hypothesis if the predicted flight duration has been overestimated in later years (see [1]), which led to 'virtual' decrease of delays.

Dataset Overview¶

We have flight databases for 1992 (5020651 entries), 1998 (5227051 entries) and 2004 (6987729 entries) for the USA. The following characteristics are taken in account:

  • Total delay which is a sum of Arrival delay and Departure delay in minutes
  • Distance in miles
  • CRS elapsed time in minutes
  • Month (between 1...12)

Distribution of Total Delay¶

  • Total delay averaged per Flight Number has unimodal distribution with no evident symmetry
  • the mode of the Total delay slightly increased with years
  • the distribution has heavy tails, especially for 1998 and 2004, which means that in 1992 99% of flights were delayed only up to 30 minutes, in 1998 and 2004 that can be an hour

def scatter_total_delay_vs_distance(frame, ax):
    mean = frame.groupby('FlightNum').mean()

Check Overestimation of Flight Duration Resulting Decrease of Delays¶

  • we check the assumption of article [1] in our conditions
  • 99% of flights have distances below 1500 miles, and estimated time for them is similar if you compare 1992, 1998 and 2004
  • the delay is not correlated with distance in any of those years (which is obvious, because the estimated time is linear dependant on distance, and we have already seen that delay isn't correlated with it
  • both parts of the assumption on our dataset are false: there's no overestimation for 99% of the flights, and the estimated time doesn't relate to delay at all
  • the assumption of the article is false on our dataset
